Learning Experience

My journey into web development has been enlightening. I started with HTML and CSS, learning how to structure web pages and style them. One challenge I faced was ensuring my pages were responsive and looked good on all devices. Using flexbox and other CSS properties was a learning curve, but it was rewarding to see the end result.

Not to forget, the professor was really cool and always there to help us out. He made CSS and HTML a lot easier to understand, which was super helpful for our assignment. Thanks to his guidance, everything seemed much more approachable and easier to handle.

Reflective Discussion

The module provided a comprehensive introduction to web development. At first, I struggled with understanding how CSS interacts with HTML elements. However, with practice and help from online resources like W3Schools, YouTube videos from Traversy Media, and Net Ninja, I gained confidence.

Design Decisions

For my portfolio design, I chose a dark background with light text to create a modern and professional look. I decided to use a black background with white font after reading that this color scheme enhances readability and reduces eye strain. I designed the overall layout myself.

I grabbed the images for the project section from Nave and Kantata. The Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn pics I found on Google. I decided to use images instead of logos because the light black background made the logos hard to see. for the menu and close icons, i used google fonts.
